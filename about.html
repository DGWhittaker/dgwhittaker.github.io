About

My name is Dominic Whittaker, and I currently work as a Senior Research Fellow with Gary Mirams at the Centre for Mathematical Medicine & Biology in the School of Mathematics at the University of Nottingham, UK. My research focuses primarily on electrophysiology and pharmacology of the heart, from the ion channel to the tissue level. In other words, I develop computer models of the electrical activity of the heart, in order to understand what causes abnormal rhythms of the heart (known as cardiac arrhythmias).

My research integrates a range of techniques from mathematical and computational modelling with experimental data from patch clamp electrophysiology and preclinical imaging. This allows me to investigate the role of therapeutic interventions and ion channel mutations in life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias, with an increasing focus on designing better experiments to inform model calibration and model selection — two aspects of model development. I am also interested in using computer models as a tool for performing drug safety risk assessment.

Before moving to Nottingham, I obtained a PhD in the Biological Physics department at the University of Manchester, UK, with a thesis entitled Pathophysiology and Pharmacology of Short QT Syndrome Gene Mutations in the Human Atria: Insights from Multi-Scale Computational Modelling. I was subsequently awarded a Wellcome Trust ISSF early career fellowship (Relating cardiac tissue structure to normal and arrhythmic excitation patterns: towards patient-specific computational models) at the University of Leeds, UK, where I worked on modelling and mapping cardiac excitation patterns in the Leeds Computational Physiology Lab.
